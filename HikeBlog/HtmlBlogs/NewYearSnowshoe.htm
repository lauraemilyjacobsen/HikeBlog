The turn of the year brought the first good weather and stable snow conditions on a weekend this winter. On 12/30/12, we invested 6 hours of driving to go to Artist Point for a snowshoe. It was three months to the day since I was up there in full autumn colors. Nature's changes are dramatic. The hillsides that were red, orange, and purple with fall foliage in September were smooth walls of white traced with ski tracks. The reflecting ponds near the ranger station were covered in several feet of ice and snow. The snow was recent enough to be smooth and undisturbed, making eerie shapes out of trees.

The snowshoe from the Heather Meadows ski area parking lot to Artist point roughly follows the road, hidden under many feet of snow. It's a short distance, but easily the most beautiful and easily accessible scenery for snowshoeing in the state.

 

Mt. Shuksan reigned over the scene. I enjoyed looking down the valley from Artist Point to find the route of the trail from the Artist Point road to Lake Ann that I took in September.

New Year's Day had a good weather forecast, so we went for our second-favorite snowshoe destination, Hurricane Ridge in the Olympics. Although the day started out clear, a high layer of white cloud drifted over the peninsula by the time we got on the trail. Not many people were on the trail (probably nursing hangovers), so there was a hush over the mountains enhanced by the still whiteness. We snowshoed up to Hurricane Hill, also a place we had visited in the summer.

Hurricane Ridge runs roughly north-south on the northern edge of the Olympic range. You get a vantage point gazing into the heart of the Olympics, one of the wildest parts of the state. Today, the early afternoon sky turned pastel pink and orange, while wisps of cloud snaked through the river valleys.
