Spending my free time hiking doesn't leave a lot of time for documenting the hikes. On Labor Day weekend, planning for the big JMT/Whitney adventure, we opted for a day hike to one of our favorites from last year, Foggy Lake and Gothic Basin. It's a gnarly, steep trail to an area closely ringed by the Mountain Loop Highway peaks and punctuated with little reflecting pools in the shadow of Del Campo Peak and the triple spires of Gothic Peak.

 

   

Did I mention this trail is steep? It's a nasty, brutish grind. But the basin is an enchanting place to explore, even on a day when the weather looked ominous. Dark clouds billowed in the gap between Gothic and Del Campo, but thankfully never settled in. The skies gradually cleared, not before giving some cool light and shadow displays in Foggy Lake.

  

We poked around to the right of Foggy Lake, noting the very first signs of fall color.

    

One of my goals for today's return to Foggy Lake was to go a ways up one of the nearby peaks. With limited time and a less-than-obvious trail up Del Campo, we boulder hopped to the opposite side of the lake and followed a boot path up the shoulder of Gothic to a fantastic vantage point.

   

 

   

A last mosey around the tarns below the basin.
